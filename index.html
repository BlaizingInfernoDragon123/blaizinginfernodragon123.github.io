Ethical concerns are one of the most critical problem areas that have emerged out of development of Artificial Intelligence and related technology. Broadly, we can divide these ethical concerns into two sub-problems-related to Data Management and other, the adoption of A.I. technology.

(i)Concerns Related To Data Management-

Data is the lifeline of A.I systems.These systems cannot exist without data.But the collection,storing and usage of data raises serious privacy concerns. Let's understant this by a simple example,i.e,Google,which uses data of its users for increasing it's revenue.One of the products offered by Google is the Android OS(Operating System). This operating system comes with a number of google apps pre-installed.So, guys if you all are using these apps and provided permissions in some instances,then Google has the following information related to you all:

  • 1.Contact List:Google knows the name and phone number of all your friends,relatives,business partners,colleagues,etc.from your phone book.Also,it knows the names of the persons with whom you like to talk and with whom you do not like to talk,and the best and worst time to call you.
  • 2.Location:Google knows the location of your house, you friend's house,houses of your relatives,your school,office , favourite mall,cinema house,etc. It also knows the time you spend in these locations,the routes you take to reach them. With a little effort it can predict if you guys own a veichle,use public transportation system,car-pool or your parent's chauffeurs you.
  • 3.E-mail:Google also knows every single mail written and received by you.It also knows about the mails that you never read and the mails that you wrote but never sent.It also knows about the job offers received by you,and about the job you were rejected from.
  • 4.Hangout:Google knows every little secret shared by or with you.It also knows about all your friends,their preferences,personal life,etc. (which was shared in hangout chats).
  • 5.Photos:All the images of you and your friends along with their names linked with the contact list;about important events like birthdays,marriage anniversaries,birth of their children,etc.Most of the time, we accept any user agreements without even realising the implications of these agreements on our privacy rights.Data related to the users is stored somewhere in some large databases.This data can be used for a number of purpose.

The information is collected in lieu of providing us with helpful services,but the potential risk here is very high.For eg, let's take the facial recognition system.The A.I system can use this technology along with the information provided by Apps like Contact and Photos,to create an extensive surveillance network that can monitor you all 24x7.

Infact if this is just a small amount of your personal data,which Google has.Think about all of the details regarding you all with Google,i.e.,your search history,internet browsing habits,phone using habits,etc.Add to the data that other companies like Microsoft,Facebook,Twitter,Amazon,etc. use. Try to imagine the extent and depth of the data that these companies have, and you all will have a clear understanding of the ethical concerns related to data collection.

I know now you all might be thinking -'What if my data gets leaked?','What if they sell sensitive information of my personal life/info. to other third party companies?'.For that let's take another example here, Many a times when we are doing online shopping in any of the sites/apps like Amazon,Flipkart,etc. in their privacy policy and terms and conditions there is often written that 'We do not send any of your credit card/bank info. to third parties and do not store this infos.' but, the there is a plot twist here they actually needs to store your infos, for future purchases or to withdraw the amount displayed on the payment page from your bank account,So this posses us a big ethical concern-'What if they withdraw money from my band account without my permission for their financial benefits?' for this cause some banks have an OTP(One Time Password) system to prevent this kind of fraud things to happen.And, there is a economical reason here to,i.e,the customer only gives them money to earn their living and they sell customer device info. to third-parties because they are getting paid for it;after all if they betray their customers then the customer will stop using them and there would be no income source then they will have no money to earn a living.For this reason, they don't do this which result in their economical loss.That's why we must always focus on safety measures before using them and should never give our credit card details to any app/site that we don't trust or are not familiar to us.

(ii)Bias In The Real-World Data

The problem here is that the A.I. system learns from the real-qorld data fed into it.This means that A.I systems can reinforce the biases found in A.I systems.Let's take a simple example here,a computer system trained on the data for last 200 years might find that more females were involved in specific jobs or that more percentage of successful buisnesses were established by mean and conclude that specificgenders are better equipped for handling certain jobs(gender bias).

Understanding or even detecting such biases is not easy because many A.I. systems actas black boxes.The reason behind their decision making is not easy or in some cases,even possible to understand.Many a times,programmers of A.I systems themselves cannot explain the logic behind decisions taken by A.I. systems.

(iii)The Problem Of Inclusion

A.I systems trained on the biased real-world data create problem of inclusion,i.e. the problem that some people are left out of the A.I decision-making system.Let's consider an example of the A.I. used by Amazon for recruitment.This created a situation in which many eligible females were left out of consideration.This is known as the problem of inclusion.

Problem of Facts and Their Interpretation

The root cores of the bias in AI systems come from the problem between facts and interpretation of facts. AI sys. can scan data and draw learnings, from them, but AI systems are not equipped to understand the reason behind a particular conclusion or learning.

Microsoft developed and launched an experimental Twitter chatbot known as 'Tay'. Tay was designed to experiment with conversational understanding. While launching Tay, Microsoft claimed that the more you chat with Tay,the smarter it will get, learning to engage people through casual and playful conversations. In less than 24 hrs of its launch,Tay learned thousands of tweets and developed a misogynistic racial perosnality.Which is kinda surprising right?
